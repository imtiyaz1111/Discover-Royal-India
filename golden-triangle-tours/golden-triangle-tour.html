Itinerary

Day 01

Delhi

Delhi: Delhi is the capital of India and its third largest city. Delhi, the seat of one of the world’s largest democracies is a combination of “Old Delhi”- the capital of Muslim India and “New Delhi” the imperial city created as the nation’s capital by the British. There’s a unique blend of tradition and modernity. Here one can see mingling of many cultures, languages, traditions and people in complete harmony.

Day 02

Delhi Sightseeing

After breakfast Proceed For Old Delhi Sightseeing Tour. Visit Raj Ghat (The mortal remains of Mahatma Gandhi were cremated on this spot on the west bank of the river Yamuna on the evening of January 31, 1948. A simple open platform inscribed with the Mahatma’s last words, ‘Hey Ram’ is set in a garden), Jama Masjid (the largest mosque in India, completed in 1656 AD by Shah Jahan), Qutub Minar (the tallest Stone tower in India), Lotus Temple (Bahá’í House of Worship), India Gate, the Parliamentary Building and the Rashtrapathi Bhavan. Overnight stay in Delhi.

Day 03

Delhi - Agra (By Train)

After breakfast Drive to Agra. Board Gatiman Express Train (DEP: 08:10 HRS, ARR: 09:45 HRS). Reach Agra, transfer to hotel and relax. Agra has been immortalized as the City of the Taj, but there is more to Agra than just the fabled monument. The older city of Agra has impressively retained much of its resplendent history, captivating every visitor with fond memories to take back home. Spend your night in the town of the most amazing wonder of the world – THE TAJ MAHAL. Overnight stay in Agra.

Day 04

Agra – Fatehpur Sikri – Jaipur

Morning at sunrise visit the magnificent Taj Mahal – one of the Seven Wonders of the World and surely the most extravagant expression of love ever created. 20,000 men laboured for over 17 years to build this memorial. Also visit Agra Fort, a rusty and majestic red-sandstone fort situated on the West Bank of the Yamuna, built by Emperor Akbar between 1565 and 1573. Visit Itmad-ud-Daulah (Baby Taj), a must-see hidden gem built by Empress Nur Jehan as a memorial to her father. Afternoon Drive to Jaipur, en-route visiting the ruined city of Fatehpur Sikri (UNESCO World Heritage Site) including Buland Darwaza. Continue drive to Jaipur, the Pink City. Overnight stay in Jaipur.

Day 05

Jaipur

After breakfast Proceed for sightseeing tour of Jaipur. Visit Amber Fort Palace (classic romantic Rajasthani fort palace completed by Jai Singh I in a beautiful fusion of Mughal and Hindu styles), City Palace (delightful blend of Mughal and Rajasthani architecture), Jantar Mantar (the largest and best preserved stone observatory built by Jai Singh II), and Hawa Mahal ( ornamental five-storey Palace of Winds with 953 niches and windows). Afternoon is free for shopping gold, silver jewellery, pottery, tie-dye silk, wooden handicrafts and carpets. Overnight stay in Jaipur.

Day 06

Jaipur – Delhi & Departure

After breakfast drive back to Delhi. Reach Delhi and transfer to airport for your connect flight home with sweet memories of the India Tour.
